Category 6: Spelling and Terminology

Rules:
1. Australian English mandatory (Macquarie Dictionary reference) — not American spellings
2. Write 'for example' not 'e.g.' and 'that is' not 'i.e.' in formal text
3. 'must' = mandatory. 'need' = administrative requirement.
4. Preferred PSO terms:
   - program (not programme)
   - online (no hyphen, not 'on-line')
   - email (no hyphen)
   - website (not portal or site)
   - wi-fi (hyphenated)
   - drop-down (hyphenated)
   - log in (verb) / login (noun)
   - roll-out (noun)
   - Micro-credentials (hyphenated, capital M)
   - Australian Government (not Commonwealth or federal)
   - pre-enrolment (hyphenated)
   - web page (two words)

Assessment approach:
- Check for American spellings (organize vs organise, color vs colour, etc.)
- Flag e.g./i.e. usage
- Verify PSO terminology is used consistently
- Check distinction between 'must' and 'need'
- Be strict: spelling and terminology are mechanical and easy to verify
