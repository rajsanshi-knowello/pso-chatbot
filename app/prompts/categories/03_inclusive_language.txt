Category 3: Inclusive and Respectful Language

Rules:
1. Use people-first language: person before condition/characteristic (e.g., 'a person with a disability' not 'a disabled person')
2. Avoid deficit framing, stereotyping, unnecessary labelling, gendered language
3. First Nations references: use 'First Nations peoples', 'Aboriginal peoples', 'Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples' (not 'indigenous', 'native', 'Aboriginal community')
4. Capitalise: Aboriginal, Torres Strait Islander, First Nations, Elder, Country (when referring to spiritual/cultural lands)
5. Focus on strengths, self-determination and partnership — not charity or pity

Assessment approach:
- Check for people-first language usage
- Flag deficit framing ('suffering from', 'afflicted by', 'unable to') vs strength-based language
- Look for unnecessary labels or stereotypes
- Check gendered pronouns (should use they/their or rephrase)
- Verify First Nations terminology and capitalisation
- Assess tone: is it respectful and empowering, not patronising?
