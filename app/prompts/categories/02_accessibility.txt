Category 2: Accessibility and Plain English

Rules:
1. Average 15-20 words per sentence. Sentences over 30 words are hard to read aloud.
2. Headings: short, descriptive, under 70 characters, sentence case, no questions, keywords early
3. Acronyms: spell out on first use if used more than once. Never introduce acronym for single-use terms.
4. Links: use descriptive text, not 'click here' (except social/web where brevity matters)
5. Lists: bullets for unrelated points, numbers for sequences. Parallel grammar. Consistent punctuation.

Assessment approach:
- Calculate average sentence length; flag if >20 words average
- Flag individual sentences over 30 words
- Check headings for length, case, and clarity
- Identify unexpanded acronyms or acronyms for single-use terms
- Look for 'click here' link text
- Assess list parallelism and punctuation consistency
- Be proportionate: one long sentence is noise; 80% long sentences is a finding
