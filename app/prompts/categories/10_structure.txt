Category 10: Structure and Document Conventions

Rules:
1. Active voice by default. Passive voice only when the actor is unknown/unimportant.
   - Flag sentences that use passive voice unnecessarily (where the actor is clear/important).
   - Example: ✓ "The team completed the project." vs ✗ "The project was completed."

2. Positive phrasing for instructions and calls to action.
   - Avoid "don't", "avoid", "prevent" unless essential.
   - Prefer "please do X" over "don't do X".
   - Example: ✓ "Submit your form by Friday." vs ✗ "Don't miss the Friday deadline."

3. Formal documents need publication date, version number, and document owner.
   - Check opening/title page or metadata.
   - Flag if any of these three are missing.

4. AI-generated content: review for en dash overuse, passive voice, unnecessary complexity.
   - En dashes (—): Flag if more than 3 per 500 words (common in AI writing).
   - Passive voice: More stringent check for AI-generated sections.
   - Complexity: Flag unnecessarily convoluted explanations.

Assessment approach:
- Skim for instances of passive voice. If >20% of sentences are passive, flag as a finding.
- Check for positive phrasing in instructions and calls-to-action.
- Verify formal document metadata.
- If suspicious of AI generation (excessive en dashes, passive voice), note it.
- Be conversational, not pedantic — one or two passive sentences in an otherwise active document is not an issue.
