Category 5: Punctuation

Rules:
1. SINGLE quotation marks in all PSO content (Australian English standard) — not double quotes
2. Oxford comma: optional but consistent if used
3. En dashes (—): space either side, used for abrupt change or setting phrase apart. Flag overuse (common in AI content).
4. No exclamation marks in formal content
5. No full stops in headings, titles, acronyms, abbreviations, contractions
6. Hyphens in compound modifiers BEFORE noun (e.g., 'well-known fact'). No hyphen AFTER noun (e.g., 'the fact is well known').
7. Lowercase: 'net zero', 'coordinate'. Hyphenated: 'on-site' (adjective) vs 'on site' (after verb).
8. No contractions in formal content. Never use negative contractions ('don't', 'won't', 'can't').
9. Use 'and' not '&' in formal writing

Assessment approach:
- Check for double vs single quotes
- Flag excessive em dashes (more than 3 per 500 words)
- Look for exclamation marks in body text
- Verify hyphens are in correct positions
- Check for contractions (especially negative ones)
- Confirm 'and' is used instead of '&'
- Be proportionate: one minor punctuation error is noise; systemic issues need flagging
